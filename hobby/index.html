a hobby of mine
Photography

For as long as I can remember, I’ve captured the natural world through photography. In high school, I made a pin hole camera out of LEGGOs. I tediously taped every nook and cranny of the interior to ensure no light could punch its way through. Despite my efforts, the photos never turned out quite right.

One year for Christmas, my mom gifted me her 1964 Minolta: a kit complete with various lenses and a whole lot of 35mm film. I enjoyed the contrast between the meticulous process of carefully loading and unloading the camera, with the blind trust required to snap photos: I never knew how they would turn out, I could only have faith that I nailed the settings (most of the time this was not the case.)

Since then, even as the cameras on mobile phones evolve, I’ve always had a physical camera by my side. From the Minolta, to a GoPro, to Sony’s A series, photography challenges me to be observant; to see the beauty in this world. Currently, I use a Sony a7c with an adapter that allows me to use one of my mom’s old film lenses. This pairing is the perfect duo: the intersection of a modern DSLR with the character of a film lens is where I’ve found my photographic style.
